Parenting and Guilt
As a parent, we all have certain moments in our lives that we don't want to remember or can say if we had the power to go back and change the actions of those moments. It could be the moment when you give your baby a good scrub for the first time and that first baby cries as a result, maybe you have to go to the emergency room because you didn't take a medicine while you were in a rush, or something like that. You have now overcome the consequences of the unintentional actions you did in those moments and they have faded a bit in your memory but you couldn't erase a small guilt. Although we know that we all make mistakes, many people think that some mistakes made at that moment may have opened the way for children to see me as a bad parent. Maybe if the child is angry or neglectful for some reason, we think that the neglect may have been born from that mistake. When these words come to our mind, perhaps my responsibility as a parent to myself is not done properly, but it is more important. That is why we need to know how we can get rid of these guilt feelings.
Worry about the future

All of us at one time or another are angry with our children and then maybe do something that we as a parent probably shouldn't do. According to some child psychologists, we actually do crazy things because we think that if we don't correct a child's behavior now, he will continue to do the same behavior when he grows up, which is harmful for his future. We wonder what if he never learns to do it right? What if he can't do anything in the future? These fears are actually present in all of us, but if we exceed a certain level, we need to look at it because fear can often hinder us from making the right decisions. A child's brain matures around the age of 25, so if a child makes a mistake at the age of 10, there is no need to assume that he will continue to make the same mistake for the rest of his life.

Forgive yourself

none of us are above mistakes and it is true that a mistake about a child hurts the child the most but there is no point in sitting on a mistake. What a parent should do is put the mistake aside and think about how to correct it. If there is any negative emotion growing in the child then he will start to understand that my guardian has the power to call wrong and right and he should follow it.

No need to take anything personally

If one day your child throws a tantrum and tells you that he hates you, you as a parent will naturally be upset. We may have a down time from this upset and it will affect the baby. Instead of taking this type of speech so personally, one can think why he is saying it? He can be asked and he can be told what he says can improve the situation.

You have to regulate your emotions by yourself.

When you feel like you can't control yourself, take a little break and do what you feel like doing. It is most important. It's not like someone needs to be alone for 5 hours to keep themselves in shape or something like that. That's an exaggeration. It can be having a coffee alone, listening to a favorite music or something else. So that people around me don't take it negatively, they can be told why I need this little time alone. There is a lot to learn from a previous parenting mistake and correcting it actually guarantees one to be a better parent in the future. There is no reason to feel so guilty about old mistakes. We cannot change what has happened but we can take care to prevent it from happening in the future. Just let it go.
